Introduction

Entropy is one of the most misunderstood concepts in science — often reduced to “disorder,” but actually much deeper. On this site, you’ll learn entropy from the simplest explanation all the way to modern scientific insights.

The Basics

At a basic level, entropy measures how many microscopic configurations a system can have. The more ways the particles can be arranged while the overall state looks the same, the higher the entropy.

The Real Story of Entropy

Entropy is not chaos, randomness, or disorder — though those ideas overlap. It is fundamentally about energy spreading out and the number of available microstates. Entropy increases when energy becomes less concentrated and more evenly distributed.
